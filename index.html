Extreme Stellar Signals Project IV

This page details the intended purpose the current round of ESSP work (ESSP IV), the most up-to-date timeline, and an optionally anonymous form for feedback/questions.

Statement of Purpose

The intended purposes of ESSP IV is as follows:
To what degree do different techniques succeed in separating out stellar signals and instrumental variation from planetary signals in a realistic observing case?

To be specific,

  • Degree: we are not attempting to answer a binary question, but rather will gauge method performance on both scale and confidence
    • How much of the signal is separated?
    • What is the expected significance of the results?
  • Separating: the focus is to separate out stellar signals to allow for a clean planet signal recovery; this is in contrast to a requirement of exactly modeling the stellar signal correctly
  • Stellar Signals: though the current round of ESSP work makes use of solar data, the goal is to understand how methods separate out stellar signals; we therefore do our best to avoid systematics unique to solar observations (e.g., differential extinction) and request that participants do their best to treat the data set as if it were of any old star
  • Realistic Observing Case: we wish to understand how methods would perform given typical night-time observing, and therefore reduce the solar data sets to the sampling and cadence typical of a blind RV survey
